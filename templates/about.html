The Story of MoMA

BORN FROM
A VISION.

"To help people understand and enjoy the visual arts of our time."

Founded in 1929 by the "Ladies"—Abby Aldrich Rockefeller, Lillie P. Bliss, and Mary Quinn Sullivan—The Museum of Modern Art was the first museum of its kind. While traditional institutions looked to the past, MoMA looked firmly at the present and the future.

The Beginning • 1929

A Revolutionary
Act.

MoMA was established as a place for the unconventional. Just nine days after the infamous stock market crash, this was a bold vision from three extraordinary women.

Today, that vision has grown into a global institution, housing over 200,000 works that continue to challenge how we see the world.
